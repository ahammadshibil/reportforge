The compounding asset. Nevo's "$700/mo with my open-source tool" got more sustained traffic than the original launch.

Template below. Fill in real numbers on Day +30. Publish on DEV.to first → cross-post to LinkedIn, Twitter, IndieHackers, and any newsletter you have.

## When to publish

**Day +30 exactly**, not Day +28 or Day +35. The "30 days" framing has psychological pull. Hit the date.

Publish at 9:00 AM ET Tuesday (any Tuesday — doesn't have to be the same day-of-week as launch). DEV.to first, then cross-post.

---

## What this post does for you

- **Compounds.** "$X MRR / 30 days after launch" gets indexed by Google for years.
- **Builds trust.** Most founders inflate or hide numbers. You don't.
- **Creates the cadence.** Day +30 → Day +60 → Day +90 → quarterly. Each one easier than the last because the format is locked in.
- **Attracts the right people.** Honest numbers attract honest builders. The ones who star "$X MRR" posts are usually the ones who later become customers / contributors.

---

## The rule for these posts

**Never lie about a metric.** Round if you want ($427 → ~$400 is fine; $427 → $1k is not). Hide if you must (e.g. you don't want to share specific paying customer count for privacy). But if you state a number, it should be exactly right.

Open-source SaaS lives or dies by trust. Trust is built one transparent number at a time.
